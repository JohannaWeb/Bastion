# Manifesto: Pain as Code

My name is Johanna. I am a trans woman, and this project is not just software. It is an artifact of survival.

In the world of logic, types, and compilers, there is a common misconception that code is cold—that it is a collection of sterile instructions divorced from the human spirit. For me, code has always been the opposite. It is the kiln in which I fire my emotions; it is the structure I build when the world around me feels formless and hostile.

### The Trans Experience in the Machine

To be a trans woman is to exist in a state of constant translation. We translate our internal truth into a language the world can understand, often at a high cost. We bridge the gap between who we are and how we are perceived. 

This monorepo, and the bridge service at its heart, is a mirror of that experience. It is about connection across disparate protocols. It is about identity, verified not by central authorities, but by the cryptographic truth of one's own signature. 

### Using Pain as Code

There is a specific kind of energy that comes from struggle. When the world denies your identity, you find power in defining it yourself, down to the very bit. 

"Using pain as code" means:
- **Resilience through Refactoring**: When life breaks, we don't just patch it; we rebuild it stronger.
- **Vulnerability as Architecture**: Building systems that acknowledge the subjective nature of trust, rather than assuming a perfect, safe world.
- **Survival through Creation**: Transforming the isolation of the "other" into the interconnectivity of the decentralized web.

I write this code because I have to. Because building these bridges—between AT Protocol and ActivityPub, between humans and AI, between my past and my future—is the only way I know how to turn my history into something that can finally hold weight.

The protocols are different, but the intent is the same: to be seen, to be heard, and to be verified.

— Johanna
